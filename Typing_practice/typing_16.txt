Local stock markets in Germany and Japan were wiped out during World War 2. Entire regions were bombed out. At the end of the war German farms only produced enough food to provide the country's citizen with 1,000 calories a day. Compare that to the US , where the stock market more than doubled from 1941 through the end of 1945, and the economy was the strongest it had been in almost two decades.

No one No one should expect them to trust the same sources of advice.
No one should expect them to agree on what maters, what's worth it, what's likely to happen next ,and what the best path forward is.
Their view of money was formed in different worlds. And when that's the case, a view about money that one group of people thinks is outrageous can make perfect sense to another.
